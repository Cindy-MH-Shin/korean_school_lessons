봄 📝 Spring

문법 Grammar + 퀴즈 Quiz
📐 핵심 문법 표현 (Key Grammar Patterns)
1~아/어지다
→ To become / To get (gradual change of state)
형용사/동사 + 아/어지다
형용사나 동사 어간 + 아/어지다
A gradual change or transformation in state. The weather "becomes" warmer, it doesn't just "become warm."
▸ 따뜻하다 → 따뜻해지다 (to become warm)
▸ 춥다 → 추워지다 (to get cold)
▸ 예쁘다 → 예뻐지다 (to become pretty)
Pattern: [Adj/Verb stem] + 아/어 + 지다
• Vowel ㅏ/ㅗ → 아지다  |  Other vowels → 어지다  |  하다 → 해지다
날씨도 많이 따뜻해졌어요.
The weather has gotten much warmer.
그럼 날이 좀 더 따뜻해지면 꼭 나가요!
Then when it gets a bit warmer, let's definitely go out!
봄이 되면 날씨가 따뜻해져요.
When spring comes, the weather becomes warm.
2~고 싶다
→ Want to do (expressing desire)
동사 어간 + 고 싶다
동사 어간 + 고 싶다
Expresses the speaker's own wish or desire. In polite form: 고 싶어요.
▸ 가다 → 가고 싶다 (want to go)
▸ 먹다 → 먹고 싶다 (want to eat)
▸ 되다 → 되고 싶다 (want to become)
Note: ~고 싶다 is for your own desire. For others' desire, use ~고 싶어하다.
봄이 오니 봄소풍 가고 싶네요.
Now that spring is here, I want to go on a picnic.
봄에는 들로 산으로 놀러 가고 싶어요.
In spring, I want to go out to the fields and mountains.
그에게로 가서 나도 그의 꽃이 되고 싶어요.
I want to go to him and become his flower too.
3~(이)라고 하지만
→ Although it's called / Even though it is said to be
명사 + (이)라고 하지만
명사 + (이)라고 하지만
Acknowledges a label or claim, then introduces a contrasting reality.
▸ After vowel: 라고 하지만  |  After consonant: 이라고 하지만
▸ 봄이라고 하지만 아직 춥다 = "It's called spring but it's still cold"
봄이라고 하지만, 아직 좀 추워요.
Even though they call it spring, it's still a bit cold.
여름이라고 하지만, 오늘은 좀 시원해요.
Even though they call it summer, it's a bit cool today.
4~아/어 보다
→ Try to do / to look / to see (by doing)
동사 어간 + 아/어 보다
동사 어간 + 아/어 보다
Two uses: (1) Try doing something, (2) Noticing/seeing by means of an action.
▸ 먹다 → 먹어 보다 (try eating)
▸ 보다 → 봐요 → 출근길에 보니... (when I looked on the commute...)
출근길에 보니 개나리가 피고 있더라고요.
Looking on my commute, I could see forsythia was blooming.
한번 먹어 봐요!
Give it a try and eat it!
이 봄나물 비빔밥을 한번 먹어 보세요.
Please try eating this spring greens bibimbap.
5~더라고요
→ I noticed that / I found that (past personal observation)
동사/형용사 어간 + 더라고요
동사/형용사 어간 + 더라고요
Used when sharing something you personally observed in the past — often with a nuance of surprise or newly discovered information. This is a first-person observational report.
Tip: ~더라고요 can only be used with YOUR OWN direct observation, not hearsay. It carries a soft "I noticed/I saw that..."
출근길에 보니 개나리가 피고 있더라고요.
On my way to work, I noticed forsythia was blooming.
구내 식당에도 비빔밥이 있더라고요.
I found out they also have bibimbap at the cafeteria.
벚꽃이 정말 아름답더라고요.
The cherry blossoms were really beautiful (I noticed).
6~때문인지 / ~(라) 그런지
→ Maybe because of / Perhaps since it's (uncertain cause)
명사 + 때문인지  |  명사 + (이)라 그런지
Expresses an uncertain or suggested cause — "I'm not 100% sure, but maybe because of..."
▸ Noun + 때문인지: maybe because of [noun]
▸ Noun + 라 그런지: maybe since it is [noun]
Both are softer / more humble than saying a definite reason.
꽃샘 추위 때문인지 좀 춥긴 해요.
Maybe because of the late cold snap, it does feel a bit cold.
환절기라 그런지 감기도 유행이네요.
Maybe because it's the change of seasons, colds are going around.
봄이라 그런지 기분이 설레네요.
Maybe because it's spring, I feel excited.

📋 문법 요약 Grammar Summary
# 패턴 Pattern 뜻 Meaning 예시 Example
1 ~아/어지다 To become / To get 따뜻해졌어요 (became warm)
2 ~고 싶다 Want to do 가고 싶네요 (I want to go)
3 ~(이)라고 하지만 Even though it's called ~ 봄이라고 하지만 (even though it's spring)
4 ~아/어 보다 Try doing / Notice by looking 출근길에 보니 (I noticed on my commute)
5 ~더라고요 I noticed / I found that 피고 있더라고요 (I noticed it was blooming)
6 ~때문인지 / ~라 그런지 Maybe because of ~ 환절기라 그런지 (maybe because it's the change of seasons)

📐 문법 퀴즈 Grammar Quiz

6가지 문법 패턴을 얼마나 이해했나요? 10문제에 도전해 보세요!

Q1
~아/어지다 의 의미는?
✅ ~아/어지다 = gradual change of state. 따뜻하다 → 따뜻해지다 (to become warm). 날씨가 따뜻해졌어요 = The weather has gotten warmer.
Q2
Complete the sentence: 봄이 오니 봄소풍 ____ 싶네요. (I want to go on a spring picnic)
✅ ~고 싶다 = want to do. Verb stem + 고 싶다: 가다 → 가고 싶다 (want to go). 봄소풍 가고 싶네요!
Q3
What grammar pattern is used in: "봄이라고 하지만, 아직 좀 추워요"?
✅ 봄이라고 하지만 = "Even though they call it spring." This pattern acknowledges a label/claim, then introduces a contrasting reality (it's still cold!).
Q4
~더라고요 를 올바르게 사용한 문장은?
✅ ~더라고요 is for sharing your own direct past observation. "피고 있더라고요" = I (personally) noticed it was blooming. It's a first-person discovery report.
Q5
Fill in the blank: 환절기____ 감기도 유행이네요. (Maybe because it's the change of seasons, colds are going around)
✅ 환절기라 그런지 = "Maybe because it's the change of seasons." ~라 그런지 expresses uncertain cause, suggesting a possible reason without being definitive.
Q6
따뜻하다 → 변화 표현 (change expression): "It became warm" 은?
✅ 따뜻하다 + ~아/어지다 → 따뜻해지다 → past polite: 따뜻해졌어요. (하다 → 해지다 pattern)
Q7
Choose the correct use of ~아/어 보다 (try to do):
✅ 먹어 봐요! = Try eating it! Verb stem 먹 + 어 봐요. This is the natural "give it a try" usage of ~아/어 보다.
Q8
~더라고요 vs. ~았어요/었어요: 차이점은?
✅ ~더라고요 carries the nuance "I (personally) noticed/discovered that..." It's used to share a newly observed fact. Compare: 비가 왔어요 (It rained) vs. 비가 오더라고요 (I noticed/saw that it was raining).
Q9
Translate: "I want to become like a flower" 꽃처럼 ____.
✅ 되다 + ~고 싶다 → 되고 싶어요 = want to become. From the poem: "그에게로 가서 나도 그의 꽃이 되고 싶어요." 🌸
Q10
Which sentence uses ~때문인지 correctly?
✅ 꽃샘 추위 때문인지 요즘 감기가 많이 걸려요 = "Maybe because of the cold snap, a lot of people are getting colds lately." ~때문인지 follows a noun directly.
