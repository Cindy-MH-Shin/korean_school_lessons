오십 원 · 50원

🌾 벼 Rice Plant
Features a rice stalk (벼), symbolizing Korea's agricultural heritage. Rice is the most important crop in Korean culture — "밥 먹었어요?" (Have you eaten?) is a common greeting!
백 원 · 100원
≈ 0.07¢ (study: 10 cents)
⚓ 이순신 Admiral Yi Sun-sin (1545–1598)
Korea's greatest naval hero. Defended Korea against Japanese invasions using the famous "Turtle Ship" (거북선). His statue stands at Gwanghwamun Plaza in Seoul.
오백 원 · 500원
≈ 0.35¢ (study: 50 cents)
🦢 두루미 Manchurian Crane
The crane (두루미) symbolizes longevity (장수) and good fortune in Korean culture. It's a national symbol of Korea, representing everlasting growth (영원한 성장).
🗣️ 숫자 읽기 Reading Coin Amounts (Sino-Korean Numbers 한자어):
十 십 (10)  |  五十 오십 (50)  |  百 백 (100)  |  五百 오백 (500)
Money uses Sino-Korean numbers (한자어 숫자). Native Korean numbers are for counting objects!

Outstanding Neo-Confucian scholar and statesman. He was born to Shin Saimdang (on ₩50,000!). Famous for proposing to train 100,000 soldiers before the Japanese invasions — a warning that was ignored. Known as "이이" (pronounced "E-E").

앞면 Front · 인물
세종대왕 King Sejong the Great
1397–1450 · 4th King of Joseon
Korea's most beloved king! Creator of Hangul (한글) in 1443, the Korean alphabet. Also advanced science, music, and agriculture. His statue stands prominently at Gwanghwamun Plaza in central Seoul. 세종대왕 is THE face of Korea.
뒷면 Back 혼천의 — A celestial globe (天球儀), symbol of scientific advancement during his reign
오만 원 · 50,000원
$50 study  |  ≈ $35.00 actual
💛
앞면 Front · 인물
신사임당 Shin Saimdang
1504–1551 · Artist, Poet & Scholar
Korea's most celebrated female artist and role model. Mother of the great scholar Yulgok Yi I (₩5,000). She excelled in painting, poetry, calligraphy, and embroidery. The first woman to appear on Korean currency (2009), symbolizing women's cultural contributions.
뒷면 Back 묵포도도 / 풍죽도 — Painting of bamboo and a plum tree (her famous works)
🧠 재미있는 연결고리 Fun Connection! Yulgok Yi I (₩5,000) is the son of Shin Saimdang (₩50,000). Mother and son are on different bills — a unique relationship in world currency! 어머니와 아들이 함께 등장해요.
⚠️ 큰 숫자 읽기 Reading Large Numbers:
Korean counts in units of 만 (10,000) — NOT thousands like English!
만 (萬) = 10,000  |  오만 (五萬) = 50,000
So ₩30,000 = 삼만 원 (3 × 10,000)  |  ₩100,000 = 십만 원 (10 × 10,000)

Money Phrases · Shopping · Paying & Asking Prices
💰 가격 묻기 · Asking Prices
💰 Price · 가격
얼마예요?
Eol-ma-ye-yo?
How much is it?
The most essential shopping phrase! Works everywhere — markets, taxis, restaurants. 얼마 = how much.
💰 Price · 가격
이거 얼마예요?
I-geo eol-ma-ye-yo?
How much is this?
이거 = this (thing). Point at any item and say this! 저거 얼마예요? = How much is that (over there)?
🏪 Market · 시장
깎아 주세요.
Kka-kka ju-se-yo.
Please give me a discount.
Use at traditional markets (전통시장) or Dongdaemun. Always smile when asking! Stores and malls don't negotiate prices.
🏪 Market · 시장
좀 더 싸게 해 주세요.
Jom deo ssa-ge hae ju-se-yo.
Please make it a little cheaper.
좀 = a little. 더 = more. 싸다 = cheap/inexpensive. A polite way to bargain without being rude!
💳 결제 · Paying
💳 Payment · 결제
카드 돼요?
Ka-deu dwae-yo?
Do you accept cards?
카드 = card. 돼요? = does it work? Korea is very card-friendly, but small restaurants (식당) sometimes only take cash (현금).
💳 Payment · 결제
현금으로 낼게요.
Hyeon-geum-eu-ro nael-ge-yo.
I'll pay in cash.
현금 = cash. 으로 = with/by (method). 낼게요 = I will pay. Sometimes cash gets you a small discount!
🧾 Change · 잔돈
거스름돈 주세요.
Geo-seu-reum-don ju-se-yo.
Please give me change.
거스름돈 = change (money returned). 잔돈 is also common. Most cashiers will give change automatically, but useful if they forget!
🧾 Receipt · 영수증
영수증 주세요.
Yeong-su-jeung ju-se-yo.
Please give me a receipt.
영수증 = receipt. 주세요 = please give. In Korea, receipts are often printed automatically, but this is handy for expense reports!
🔢 금액 말하기 · Saying Amounts
🔢 Amount · 금액
천 원이에요.
Cheon wo-ni-e-yo.
It's 1,000 won.
천 = 1,000 (Sino-Korean). 원 = won. 이에요 = it is. Pattern: [amount]원이에요. Try: 오만 원이에요 (It's 50,000 won).
🔢 Amount · 금액
합계가 얼마예요?
Hap-gye-ga eol-ma-ye-yo?
What's the total?
합계 = total sum. 가 = subject particle. Very useful when buying multiple items. You can also just say "전부 얼마예요?" (altogether how much?).
🔢 Amount · 금액
너무 비싸요.
Neo-mu bi-ssa-yo.
It's too expensive!
너무 = too (much). 비싸다 = expensive. The opposite: 싸요 (it's cheap). 적당해요 = it's reasonable! Great for markets.
💵 ATM · 현금기
ATM 어디 있어요?
ATM eo-di i-sseo-yo?
Where is the ATM?
어디 = where. 있어요? = is there? GS25, CU, and 7-Eleven convenience stores all have ATMs that accept foreign cards (해외 카드 가능)!
📱 한국의 디지털 결제 Korea's Digital Payment Culture:
Korea is one of the world's most cashless societies! Common payment methods:
💳 신용카드 (credit card)  |  📱 카카오페이 (KakaoPay)  |  📱 삼성페이 (Samsung Pay)  |  🚌 교통카드 T머니 (transit card)
Always have some 현금 (cash) for small restaurants and traditional markets!

💰 돈 퀴즈 · Money Quiz

한국 돈에 대해 얼마나 알고 있나요? Test your knowledge of Korean won!

Q1 · 동전 Coins
100원 동전에 있는 인물은 누구예요?
(Who is on the 100 won coin?)
✅ 이순신 (Yi Sun-sin) is on the 100원 coin! He was a great naval admiral who defended Korea from Japan using the Turtle Ship (거북선). His statue is at Gwanghwamun Plaza in Seoul.
Q2 · 지폐 Banknotes
한글을 창제한 세종대왕은 어떤 지폐에 있나요?
(King Sejong, who created Hangul, is on which banknote?)
✅ 세종대왕 is on the 만 원 (₩10,000) note — the most used banknote in Korea! He created Hangul in 1443 and is Korea's most beloved historical figure.
Q3 · 지폐 Banknotes
오만원권(₩50,000)에 있는 인물은 누구예요? (Who is on the ₩50,000 note?)
✅ 신사임당 Shin Saimdang (1504–1551) is on ₩50,000 — the first woman on Korean currency! She was a brilliant artist and scholar, and mother of Yulgok Yi I who is on the ₩5,000 note. 어머니와 아들! 💛
Q4 · 환율 Exchange Rate
실제 환율로 (2025년 기준) 만원권(₩10,000)은 미국 달러로 얼마 정도예요?
(At the actual 2025 exchange rate, ₩10,000 is approximately how many US dollars?)
✅ At the real 2025 rate (~$1 = ₩1,430), ₩10,000 ≈ $7.00. Quick trick: divide won amount by 1,400 for dollars. So that ₩15,000 삼겹살 meal costs about $10.50 in real terms!
Q5 · 숫자 Numbers
"₩30,000" 을 한국어로 바르게 읽은 것은?
(Which correctly reads ₩30,000 in Korean?)
✅ 삼만 원 = 3 × 만(10,000) = 30,000. Korean counts in units of 만 (10,000), not thousands! So 20,000 = 이만 원, 50,000 = 오만 원. This is different from English!
Q6 · 쇼핑 Shopping Phrase
시장에서 가격을 깎아달라고 할 때 쓰는 표현은?
(Which phrase asks for a discount at a market?)
✅ 깎아 주세요 = "Please give me a discount!" 깎다 = to cut/reduce. Use at traditional markets (전통시장) like Namdaemun or Dongdaemun with a smile! 😊
Q7 · 동전 Coins
50원 동전에 그려진 그림은 무엇인가요?
(What image is on the 50 won coin?)
✅ The 50원 coin features 벼 (rice plant) — representing Korea's agricultural heritage. Rice is central to Korean culture! 밥 (cooked rice/meal) is so important that "밥 먹었어요?" (Have you eaten?) is used as a greeting. 🌾
